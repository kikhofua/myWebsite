About

Kamoya is an enthusiastic learner and a keen observer of good design. His interest in the visual arts can be traced back to his infancy when he would draw on nearly everything, including the walls of his former home in Lagos, Nigeria. Nevertheless, his academic interests are mainly in the sciences.

He is a second year student at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he plans to earn a Bachelors Degree in Computer Science in 2018. Aside from his studies at MIT, Kamoya enjoys playing chess with others, especially his superiors so that he might strengthen his play. He also enjoys playing soccer and table-tennis, singing or rapping along to his favorite songs, and exercising at the gym early in the morning, amongst other things.

Furthermore, he loves to draw inspiration from great people across many works of life. He appreciates the support and sacrifices of his parents and they inspire him to do great things. He and his two friends/teammates recently built Yarn, for which they won the Audience Choice Award and came 2nd overall in the main division of the 2016 MIT 6.148 Web Programming Competition. Kamoya is currently working on a graphic illustration (pictured below) and beefing up his skills in full stack web-app development.
